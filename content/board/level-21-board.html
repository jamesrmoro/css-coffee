The coffee cups are spread across the table, but the saucers are grouped at the top. You can use align-content to define how multiple lines should be spaced from each other. This property accepts the following values:

  • flex-start: Lines are grouped at the top of the container.
  • flex-end: Lines are grouped at the bottom of the container.
  • center: Lines are grouped at the vertical center of the container.
  • space-between: Lines are positioned with equal space between them.
  • space-around: Lines are positioned with equal space around them.
  • stretch: Lines stretch to fill the container.

This can be confusing, but align-content determines the spacing between lines, while align-items determines how the lines as a whole are aligned within the container. When there is only one line, align-content has no effect.

.table {
	display: flex;
	flex-wrap: wrap;
}
